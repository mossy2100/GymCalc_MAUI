Introduction

Thanks for trying GymCalc :)

Sometimes, especially when working with barbells, it is not obvious what plates you should be adding to the bar to get the total weight you want, especially once you start doing pyramid or reverse pyramid training. GymCalc solves that problem for you.

Review the Instructions to learn how to use the app.

Weights

I've included the usual weights for bars, plates, and dumbbells found in most gyms (at least, most gyms in Australia). Regarding bar weights, 20kg is the standard and by far the most common, although some gyms may also have 15kg or 25kg bars. EZ-curl bars are less standardised and usually in the 7.5-10kg range.

Most gyms don't have so-called "micro" or fractional plates (those in the 0.25-1 kg/lb range), but they can be helpful when you only want to increase the weight by a small amount. With this app, they can be very helpful for getting closer to the ideal weight.

I haven't taken into consideration weights of dumbbell bars, as this is highly variable.

Colours

The colours for plates come from the standard colours for Olympic bumper plates. For kettlebells, official competition colours have been used. Other colours have been used for non-standard weights. For plates, I've made multiples of 10 the same colour. You can modify the colours as you please to suit the weights in your gym.
